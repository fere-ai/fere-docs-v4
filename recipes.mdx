Recipes are pre-built, ready-to-run workflows that help you get the most out of Fere without writing a single message. Pick one, set a few parameters, and hit run.

## What Are Recipes?

Think of recipes as starter templates. Each one packages a common task — like finding trending tokens, dollar-cost averaging, or buying a token on a specific chain — into a one-click experience. You pick a recipe, set options like which chain or how many tokens, and Fere runs it.

They're designed to help new users see what Fere can do **before diving into writing complex strategies themselves**. But even power users love them for quick, repeatable tasks.

You'll find recipe cards right on the Home screen, below the main input bar. You can also browse the full library by clicking **"View Recipe Library"**.

## How to Use a Recipe

<Steps>
  <Step title="Browse Recipes">
    See the available recipes on your Home screen or the Recipe Library — each one explains what it does.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure">
    Set your parameters — which chain, how many results, budget, frequency, etc.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run">
    Hit run and watch Fere execute. The agent creates a plan, runs each task, and returns results.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Act on Results">
    Review the output — like a list of trending tokens with charts — and take action from there.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## From Recipes to Power Strategies

Recipes are training wheels — really good ones. Once you see how Fere breaks down a "Trending Tokens" recipe into subtasks, you'll start to understand the building blocks, and before long you'll be writing messages like:

```
Every morning, scan Base and Solana for tokens that gained
30% overnight. Cross-reference with Twitter sentiment. If any
score above 85, buy $10 worth and set TP at +50%, SL at -15%.
```

That's the power of Fere — recipes get you started, your imagination takes you further.
