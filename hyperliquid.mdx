## What Is This?

Hyperliquid is a decentralized perpetual futures venue. You trade BTC, ETH, SOL, and 100+ other assets with leverage, funding rates, and on-chain settlement — without leaving Fere.

Fere handles the hard parts: linking an API trading key to your wallet, bridging USDC into HyperCore via Bridge2, executing orders through chat, and withdrawing back to any supported chain. Your **EVM wallet address is your Hyperliquid account** — there is no separate signup.

## Minimum Amounts & Fees

| Item | Amount | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Minimum deposit (Fere)** | **$50 USD** | Enforced in the Fund Hyperliquid modal and API. |
| **Bridge2 protocol floor** | **$5 USDC** | Hyperliquid's on-chain minimum per Bridge2 transfer. Fere's product minimum is higher ($50) for safety. |
| **First-deposit activation** | **~$1 USDC** | HyperCore deducts a one-time activation fee on the first inbound transfer to a new EOA. Your tradable balance is slightly less than the gross deposit. |
| **Minimum withdraw** | **$6 USD gross** | **$1** Hyperliquid Bridge2 fee + **$5** minimum net to Arbitrum. You cannot withdraw your exact full balance — leave room for the fee. |
| **Minimum perp trade (chat)** | **~$10.50 notional** | `size × price` must meet this before the agent opens a position (small slippage buffer). |

<Warning>
  **Do not send less than $5 to Bridge2 directly.** Deposits below Hyperliquid's protocol minimum can be lost. Always use Fere's **Fund Hyperliquid** flow so we enforce the correct floors.
</Warning>

## Setup — Enable, Then Fund

Hyperliquid trading is a two-step flow: link the API wallet, then add USDC collateral.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Fund Hyperliquid">
    Launch the setup modal from any of these entry points:

    - **Settings → Day Trading** or **Swing Trading** — tap **Fund Hyperliquid** on the banner
    - **Wallet panel → Assets** — when prompted for Hyperliquid collateral (strategy or trading flows)

    The modal opens to **Enable** if you have not completed setup yet, or straight to **Fund** if the API wallet is already linked.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Hyperliquid trading">
    Click **Enable**. Fere queues a background task that registers a Fere-managed API wallet with Hyperliquid. This usually takes about a minute.

    If setup fails with a "not funded" error, continue to the fund step first — some accounts need an initial Bridge2 deposit before the API key can be approved.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fund your perp account">
    On the **Fund Hyperliquid** screen:

    1. **Pay with** — pick any token from your holdings on a supported chain (Polymarket cash is excluded).
    2. **Amount** — enter at least **$50 USD**. The UI shows a conversion preview for your selected token.
    3. Confirm — Fere swaps to native Arbitrum USDC, sends it to Hyperliquid Bridge2, and waits for HyperCore credit.

    After the bridge confirms, Fere may run a chained **approve agent** step if this was your first fund. You'll see "Hyperliquid funded and ready to trade" when complete.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify in Settings (optional)">
    Go to **Settings → Hyperliquid** to confirm:

    - Account activated on Hyperliquid
    - Fere setup complete
    - **Trading keys** list (Fere-managed keys vs any external keys on the same address)

    Use **Re-run setup** if the key is missing or **Rotate** to refresh a Fere-managed key.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  **First deposit timing.** Bridge2 credits typically land in **1–3 minutes**. The fund modal keeps polling until your account is ready. Withdrawals can take **several minutes** end-to-end (Hyperliquid settlement + optional swap to your destination chain).
</Info>

## Withdraw — From HyperCore Back to Your Wallet

Withdrawals are done from the **wallet panel**, not chat.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find USDC (Hyperliquid) in Assets">
    Open the wallet panel → **Assets**. Look for **USDC (Hyperliquid)** — this is your HyperCore collateral balance, not an open perp position.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Sell / Withdraw">
    Click **Sell** on the USDC (Hyperliquid) row. The modal title reads **Withdraw from Hyperliquid**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose amount and destination">
    - Enter at least **$6 USD** (covers the **$1** Hyperliquid fee and **$5** net minimum).
    - Pick a **destination chain and token** (e.g. USDC on Base or Ethereum). Fere bridges from HyperCore → Arbitrum, then swaps if needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and wait">
    Settlement can take a few minutes. Do not close the modal while the task is in progress — holdings may refresh mid-flow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To **close an open perp** (not withdraw collateral), use **Sell** on the perp row in Assets (e.g. BTC on Hyperliquid) or ask the agent in chat to close the position.

## Trade via Chat

Once funded, talk to the agent like you would a trading desk. The **perp_trading** skill handles Hyperliquid execution — opens, closes, leverage, TP/SL, funding analysis, and order management.

### What the agent can do

| You ask for… | What happens |
| --- | --- |
| Open a long or short | Confirms size, side, and leverage (default **1× cross**), then submits a market order |
| Close a position | Full or partial close via `close_perp_position` |
| Take profit / stop loss on an **existing** position | Attaches reduce-only triggers — **not** a second position |
| Scale out at a limit price | Places a reduce-only limit order at your target |
| Change leverage or margin mode | Updates cross vs isolated per asset |
| Check funding rates | Surfaces who pays whom before you open |
| List positions & PnL | Reads holdings filtered to `protocol == "Hyperliquid"` |

### Example prompts

```
Long $100 of BTC at 2x on Hyperliquid
```

```
What's my Hyperliquid ETH position?
```

```
Set take profit at $95,000 and stop loss at $88,000 on my BTC perp
```

```

```

```
Show funding rates for BTC and ETH
```

```
Sell half my DOGE perp at $0.18
```

<Warning>
  **Leverage risk.** The agent warns on leverage above **10×**. Liquidation is real — confirm size and leverage before executing large positions.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  **Fund and withdraw stay in the UI.** Deposits and Bridge2 withdrawals go through **Fund Hyperliquid** and the wallet **Sell** flow. Chat is for **trading** — opens, closes, TP/SL, and market analysis — not for moving collateral in or out.
</Tip>

### How Hyperliquid balances show up in your wallet

The wallet panel sources Hyperliquid rows from your account state on HyperCore. Each value reflects your **actual stake**, not the leveraged notional, so totals match real exposure:

| Row | What it shows | How it's computed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **USDC (Hyperliquid)** | Free cash you can withdraw or trade with | Perp `withdrawable` + free spot USDC (`total − hold`). Excludes short-sale proceeds sitting in `totalRawUsd` and USDC locked in open spot orders. |
| **{COIN}-PERP** row | Your equity in that open position | `marginUsed + unrealizedPnL`. Position size and mark price are still surfaced via the size and price fields; multiply for notional. |

Implications:

- The portfolio sum equals **`accountValue` (perp equity) + free spot USDC**, not the leveraged notional sum.
- An underwater cross-margin position whose `|unrealizedPnL| > marginUsed` can momentarily show a **negative position value** before HL's liquidation engine fires. This is expected — the row reflects real-time stake.
- USDC locked in open spot limit orders (`hold`) is intentionally excluded from cash. If you cancel those orders, the freed USDC will appear on the next refresh.

## Automated Strategies

**Day Trading** and **Swing Trading** under Settings run scheduled checks against your Hyperliquid account. Each shows a **Fund Hyperliquid** CTA until setup and collateral are in place.

These strategies use the same API wallet and collateral as manual chat trading. Keep enough USDC on HyperCore for margin, and review open positions in the wallet panel or by asking the agent.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Setup failed — add funds first" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Error code **HYPERLIQUID_NOT_FUNDED** means Hyperliquid needs an initial Bridge2 deposit before the API wallet can be approved. Complete **Fund Hyperliquid** ($50+), then tap **Re-run setup** in Settings.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Deposit below $50 rejected" icon="dollar-sign">
    Fere enforces a **$50 minimum** deposit. Hyperliquid's Bridge2 accepts $5 on-chain, but smaller amounts through Fere are blocked to avoid failed or uneconomical flows.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Withdraw below $6 rejected" icon="arrow-up-from-line">
    Minimum gross withdraw is **$6** — **$1** goes to Hyperliquid's Bridge2 fee; at least **$5** must reach Arbitrum. Try a higher amount or leave collateral on HyperCore for trading.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Chat says trade is too small" icon="chart-line">
    Perp opens need **~$10.50 notional** (`size × price`). Ask for a larger notional (e.g. "$15 of BTC") or increase leverage after confirming risk.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Position not showing after chat trade" icon="clock">
    Hyperliquid writes are async. Wait **~5 seconds** and ask again, or refresh holdings in the wallet panel. The agent may say "submitted" until read-back confirms the fill.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="External API key on my address" icon="key">
    Settings lists **all** API wallets Hyperliquid recognizes for your EOA. Non-Fere keys show a warning. Fere cannot revoke third-party keys surgically — use **Rotate** on Fere-managed keys only, or manage external bots directly on Hyperliquid.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
